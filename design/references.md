# PawPlan — References

Curated references that anchor direction. Every new asset or surface should be able to cite one.

| Source | What to steal |
|---|---|
| [linear.app](https://linear.app) | Typographic density that reads as craft — tight line-height on data labels, generous negative space in structural zones, nothing decorative that doesn't also orient. |
| [stripe.com/dashboard](https://stripe.com) | The gold standard for making financial data feel trustworthy. Monospaced numbers in context, muted palette with one confident accent, grid discipline without visible borders. |
| [anthropic.com](https://anthropic.com) | Editorial restraint — voice as visual, brand as product. The page does not need illustration to feel considered; what it refuses to say is part of the positioning. |
| [read.cv](https://read.cv) | Typography-led asymmetric layout. Left-rail text anchoring, generous leading, document feel rather than app feel. |
| [vercel.com](https://vercel.com) | Typography-led product surface. Dense information that still breathes. Metric labels sit at smaller weight below the stat, not above. |
| [mercury.com](https://mercury.com) | Small-business banking that feels modern without feeling startup-cute. The balance of warmth and rigor in copy and layout PawPlan is chasing. |
| [bench.co](https://bench.co) | Bookkeeping SaaS that earns trust with independent owners. Clean data surfaces and photography of real business owners — not stock smiles. |

## Anti-references — studied to avoid

| Source | Why it's here |
|---|---|
| Banfield / VCA marketing surfaces | Corporate-warm register — forced delight, stock pet photography, discount-sheet layouts. PawPlan's voice and visual system are defined in contrast. |
| Generic pet-wellness apps | Paw-print patterns, pastel-lavender palettes, "join thousands of clinics" social proof. The register of a Squarespace template for a pet groomer. |

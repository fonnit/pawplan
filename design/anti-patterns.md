# PawPlan — Anti-Patterns

The dead list. Specific LLM-default looks to refuse. Every item scoped to PawPlan — not generic design advice.

## No Tailwind teal-500, no default Tailwind neutral ramp
Teal is the locked `oklch(0.52 0.13 196)` token. `#14b8a6` appears in 40% of "modern SaaS" defaults and instantly erases the brand. Neutrals are warm (paper/ink tokens), not cool Tailwind gray.

## No paw-print motif beyond the `logo-mark`
The logo mark is the single paw-adjacent element. Literal paw prints in empty states, dividers, loaders, bullet markers, or decorative filler read as Squarespace pet-groomer clipart.

## No gradient hero overlays
The `enrollment-hero` is a photograph on cream, not a tinted wash. Linear or radial gradients over the raster kill the print-object premise instantly.

## No rounded, drop-shadowed stat cards on the dashboard
Metrics live on the paper surface, separated by rules and type weight. Lifted `rounded-xl` cards with `box-shadow` read as onboarding-screen decoration, not an instrument-grade dashboard.

## No centered hero + three feature cards + footer CTA
The generic AI-app shape the asymmetry principle explicitly rejects. Hero is left-anchored, raster bleeds right, no three-card feature grid anywhere.

## No pure white (#FFFFFF) surfaces
Paper is warm (`--paper`, `#FAF8F5`). White anywhere reads as a browser default, not a considered surface.

## No "Join thousands of clinics" social-proof row
PawPlan is early. False-scale badge rows destroy earned trust. The break-even math is the proof; no aggregate counters, no logo walls.

## No sans typeface for financial figures
Every MRR, ARR, break-even, Stripe amount, percentage, and member count renders in Berkeley Mono. Using Inter for numbers collapses the distinction between the operational spine and prose — and erases the signature.
